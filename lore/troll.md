# Troll — D&D Lore Research

## Origin / History

**Real-world roots:** The name comes from Norse/Scandinavian folklore, but the resemblance largely ends there. Traditional Scandinavian trolls varied wildly — enormous dim-witted mountain dwellers, small mischievous forest creatures, beings that turn to stone in sunlight.

**The actual source:** The D&D Troll owes far more to Poul Anderson's 1961 novel *Three Hearts and Three Lions* than to Norse myth. Anderson described a tall, rubbery, green-skinned monster with remarkable regenerative powers that could only be stopped by fire. Gygax and Arneson adopted this concept wholesale for original D&D (1974). The regeneration-plus-fire-vulnerability combo has been the defining signature since the first Monster Manual (1977).

So while the *name* is Norse, the D&D Troll is really an American fantasy creation inspired by a mid-20th-century novel.

## Appearance

- **Size:** Large. ~9 feet tall (~2.7m), up to 9.5 feet.
- **Weight:** ~500 pounds (~225 kg) — lean and gangly, not bulky.
- **Build:** Lanky, disproportionately long arms that can drag near the ground. Thin but ropy with wiry muscle. Hunched, stooped posture.
- **Skin:** Rubbery, thick hide. Moss green to dark green, sometimes grey or brownish tones. Rough, pebbly texture. Elastic quality tied to regeneration.
- **Hair:** Sparse, stringy, typically dark (black or dark green). Often matted and filthy.
- **Face:** Long pointed nose. Small beady eyes with a dull hungry gleam. Wide mouth full of sharp uneven fangs. Pointed ragged ears.
- **Hands/Claws:** Large hands with long sharp claws — primary weapons.
- **Smell:** Notoriously foul-smelling — rotting meat and swamp decay.
- **Posture:** Despite height, they hunch and lope rather than stand fully upright.
- **Blood:** Dark green or greenish-black.

## Behavior

- **Intelligence (INT 7):** Below average human but not animal-level. Can speak Giant, set basic ambushes, remember grudges. Crude, impulsive, driven primarily by hunger.
- **Ravenously hungry** — hunger is the dominant motivator. Almost always looking for food.
- **Aggressive and fearless** — regeneration makes them recklessly confident. Charge into combat without hesitation knowing they can heal.
- **Solitary or small groups** — not social creatures. Live alone or in small family groups. Don't build civilizations.
- **Territorial** — claim hunting grounds, attack anything that enters.
- **Chaotic Evil** but more feral brutality than calculated cruelty — apex predators following instinct.
- **Can be bullied into service** — stronger creatures (hags, giants) sometimes subjugate trolls as guards using fire to keep them in line.
- **Panicked by fire** — one of the few things that genuinely frightens them.

---

## Habitat

- **Primary:** Swamps, marshes, bogs, and dark forests. "Dark, damp places."
- **Underground:** Caves, ruins, dungeon environments.
- **Climate:** Cold or temperate. Rarely found in deserts or tropics.
- **Lairs:** Don't build structures. Occupy natural caves, hollow trees, ruined buildings, or nestle into swamp muck. Lairs are filthy, littered with bones and refuse.
- **Under bridges:** Some D&D sources note trolls lurking near bridges and river crossings to ambush travelers — a nod to folklore.

---

## Diet

- **Omnivorous, prefer meat.** Will eat almost anything organic.
- **Voracious appetite** — regeneration requires enormous caloric intake. Eat constantly, almost never satisfied.
- **Not picky:** Animals, humanoids, carrion, vegetation in a pinch. Do not cook food.
- **Cannibalistic** when desperate enough.
- **Hunting style:** Ambush predators. Lurk in swamps, near paths, in dark forests. Rely on speed, strength, and confidence in regeneration.
- **Bone piles:** Lairs identifiable by heaps of gnawed bones.
- **Stomach acid** is incredibly powerful — can digest almost anything.

## Abilities / Combat (5e SRD)

### Regeneration — THE Defining Feature
- Heals **10 HP at the start of each turn.** Relentless.
- **Severed limbs regrow.** Severed pieces wriggle independently and try to reattach. A severed hand will crawl across the ground like a spider. In some lore, a severed piece can grow into a new troll.
- **Cannot die from normal damage alone.** Reduced to 0 HP by physical damage? Just starts regenerating and gets back up.
- **Stops ONLY with fire or acid damage.** If it takes fire/acid, regeneration is suppressed that round. If dropped to 0 HP without regenerating, it dies.

### Stats
- **CR 5** (1,800 XP)
- **AC:** 15 (natural armor)
- **HP:** 84 (8d10 + 40)
- **Speed:** 30 ft
- **Multiattack:** Three attacks per turn — one bite, two claws
  - Bite: +7, 1d6+4 piercing
  - Claws: +7, 2d6+4 slashing each
- **STR 18, DEX 13, CON 20** — very strong, extremely tough
- **Darkvision:** 60 ft
- **Keen Smell:** Advantage on Perception checks by smell. Track prey like a bloodhound.

---

## Weaknesses

1. **Fire** — primary weakness. Prevents regeneration. Trolls instinctively fear fire.
2. **Acid** — functions identically to fire for stopping regeneration.
3. **Low intelligence** — can be tricked, lured into traps, outsmarted.
4. **No ranged attacks** — purely melee.
5. **Solitary** — rarely coordinate with allies, easy to isolate.
6. **Hunger as a lever** — insatiable hunger can be exploited with bait.

## Ecology / Variants

### Variants
- **Dire Troll:** Larger, more powerful, sometimes mutated by magical exposure.
- **Rot Troll:** Regeneration corrupted by necrotic energy. Decaying flesh that still regenerates.
- **Spirit Troll:** Incorporeal, can phase through walls.
- **Venom Troll:** Secretes toxic venom, attacks poison victims.
- **Two-Headed Troll:** Rare mutation — harder to surprise. Sometimes result of imperfect regeneration (lost head, grew two back).
- **Ice/Snow Troll:** Adapted to cold environments. White or blue-skinned.
- **Scrags (Aquatic Trolls):** Water-adapted. Can only regenerate in contact with water.

### Reproduction & Lifespan
- Reproduce normally (not through regeneration splitting despite fan theories).
- Lifespan potentially very long — regeneration means they don't age normally. Die from fire, acid, or starvation rather than old age.
- Young trolls grow quickly, driven from parent's territory once self-sufficient.

### Ecological Role
- Apex predators in their habitats. Keep prey populations in check.
- Lairs create "dead zones" other creatures avoid.
- Sometimes enslaved by more powerful creatures (hill giants, hags) using fire for obedience.

## Interesting Facts

1. **Severed parts crawl back.** Cut off a hand and it crawls across the ground like a spider to reattach. Cut one in half and both halves try to regenerate.
2. **Basically indestructible without fire or acid.** The classic adventurer's lesson: "Always bring a torch when troll-hunting."
3. **They eat EVERYTHING.** Rocks, wood, dirt when desperate. Stomach acid dissolves almost anything.
4. **In D&D since 1974.** Barely changed in 50 years — perfectly designed concept.
5. **Can smell you coming.** Keen scent means hiding from a troll is very difficult.
6. **Some live under bridges!** Like the fairy tale, but the D&D version is much scarier than Three Billy Goats Gruff.
7. **Feel less pain.** Fight with wild abandon because injuries are temporary inconveniences.
8. **Green blood.** Dark green or greenish-black.

## Recommended Reveal Sections

1. **"What are they afraid of?" (Weaknesses)** — Fire and acid. THE iconic troll fact. Kids love learning the "trick" to beating them.
2. **"Can they really grow back?" (Regeneration deep-dive)** — The crawling-hand detail is the single most fascinating thing about trolls for kids.
