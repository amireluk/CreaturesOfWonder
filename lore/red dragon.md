# Red Dragon — Research Notes

## D&D Stats & Mechanics
- **CR**: 17 (adult), 24 (ancient) — one of the highest-CR creatures in the game
- **Size**: Huge (adult), Gargantuan (ancient)
- **Habitat**: Mountains, volcanoes — the hotter and more inhospitable the better
- **Danger**: 5/5 — a landmark of "legendary threat"
- **Key abilities**:
  - Fire breath: 60-ft cone (adult), DC 21 Dex save, 18d10 fire damage
  - Frightful Presence: creatures within 120 ft must succeed Wis save or be frightened for 1 minute
  - Legendary Resistance (3/day): can succeed on failed saves
  - Legendary Actions: can act outside its turn
  - Immunities: fire damage, charmed, frightened
- **Intelligence**: 16 — genuinely smart, tactical in combat
- **Charisma**: 19 — commanding and persuasive when it suits them
- **Lifespan**: Centuries; grows more powerful with age (wyrmling → young → adult → ancient)

## Behavior & Ecology
- Supremely arrogant — considers itself the apex of all creation, the rightful ruler of everything it surveys
- Highly territorial; will attack anything that enters its domain without paying tribute
- **Obsessive hoarders**: collects gold, gems, magic items, and anything shiny or valuable. Knows its entire hoard by memory. Missing a single coin is enough to trigger a rampage.
- Sleeps on top of its treasure pile in the deepest chamber of its volcanic lair
- Lone creatures — adults do not tolerate other dragons in their territory
- Will subjugate nearby humanoid settlements as servants, demanding tribute and food
- Not just brutish — red dragons are patient and strategic; will study an enemy before striking

## Real-World Origins
- Dragons appear in nearly every world culture: European fire-breathing dragons, Norse Fáfnir, Norse Nidhogg
- European folklore: the dragon is evil, hoards treasure, must be slain by a hero (St. George, Beowulf)
- The D&D red dragon synthesizes the worst traits of Western dragon tradition: fire, greed, pride, and total contempt for humans
- The "chromatic" classification (red, blue, black, green, white) is original to D&D — no direct mythology parallel

## Interesting Facts
- A red dragon's internal temperature is so high that its body heat can be felt from 30 feet away
- Ancient red dragons can understand dozens of languages and often collect books and scrolls alongside gold
- Red dragons are the most likely to demand tribute from cities rather than simply destroy them — they find servitude more satisfying than wanton destruction
- A red dragon's lair slowly heats the surrounding rock over decades; nearby springs become hot springs, caves become warm, wildlife adapts or leaves
- Their eyes can see perfectly in total darkness and in the blinding smoke of their own fire

## Reveal Section Candidates

1. **`lair`** — The volcanic lair is iconic, visually dramatic, and directly tied to their behavior (sleeping on gold). Perfect for revealing the obsessive hoarder aspect. **→ RECOMMENDED**
2. **`abilities`** — Fire breath is the defining ability and the most surprising in terms of scale and power. Worth explaining to kids how it actually works and how devastating it is. **→ RECOMMENDED**
3. **`scale`** — Could be used to communicate sheer size (bigger than a bus, wingspan wider than a soccer goal), but this is better handled visually.

**Recommendation**: `lair` + `abilities` (fire breath). These are the two most essential things to know about a red dragon.
